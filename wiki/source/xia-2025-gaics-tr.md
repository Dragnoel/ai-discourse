Peer-reviewed scale development and validation article in the *Journal of Computers in Education* (Springer, 2025). Qi Xia and colleagues from nine universities developed and validated the GAICS-TR, the first scale specifically designed to measure Generative AI Competency Self-Efficacy for Teachers and Researchers in higher education. N=787 academics across nine universities participated in confirmatory factor analysis and group comparison studies.

## Summary

Existing digital and AI competency frameworks for educators have generally addressed K-12 contexts or traditional digital technologies, leaving a gap in validated instruments for higher education academics who must integrate GenAI into both teaching and research activities. Xia et al. argue that the dual responsibilities of HE academics (teaching and research) create unique competency demands that existing frameworks do not adequately address, particularly in the ethical, methodological, and assessment dimensions of GenAI use.

The GAICS-TR scale is built on a proposed four-domain, nine-dimension framework derived from literature review and expert consultation. The four domains are: basic GenAI understanding (conceptual and technical foundations); teaching with GenAI (applying GenAI in instructional design, assessment, and student support); research with GenAI (applying GenAI in literature search, data analysis, writing support, and methodology); and professional engagement with GenAI (continuing professional development, ethical practice, collaboration). The nine dimensions refine these domains into specific competency areas.

Scale validation with N=787 academics across nine universities used confirmatory factor analysis. The factor structure was confirmed and group comparison showed no significant differences across gender or age, suggesting the scale measures a general academic GenAI competency profile rather than one specific to demographic subgroups. The findings contribute a validated measurement instrument that addresses a documented gap in the field and enable future research on GenAI competency development in HE.
