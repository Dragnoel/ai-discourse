Peer-reviewed empirical article in *Education and Information Technologies* (Springer, 2025). Domingo Verano-Tacoronte, Alicia Bolívar-Cruz, and Silvia Sosa-Cabrera (Spanish public universities) report a PLS-SEM structural equation modelling study of N=249 faculty members examining how three distinct types of technology-related anxiety shape behavioral intention to adopt ChatGPT, mediated by effort expectancy and performance expectancy from the UTAUT framework.

## Summary

Prior research on technology adoption has attended primarily to rational-cognitive barriers (perceived usefulness, ease of use) and to social influence, with anxiety treated as a residual or undifferentiated construct. Verano-Tacoronte et al. argue that in the context of GenAI specifically, anxiety is multidimensional in ways that standard adoption models do not capture, and that different anxiety types operate through different mechanisms to influence adoption intention.

The study distinguishes three types of anxiety: anxiety about the future of the academic profession (concern about ChatGPT driving significant professional role changes); anxiety about personal misuse of ChatGPT (concerns about one's own ability to manage and use the tool appropriately); and anxiety about negative impacts on student learning (concerns about ChatGPT diminishing student effort and dependency). Surveys were administered to 249 faculty members from Spanish public universities. PLS-SEM was used to assess the direct effects of each anxiety type on behavioral intention and the mediating roles of effort expectancy (EE) and performance expectancy (PE) from UTAUT.

Anxiety about student learning showed a significant direct negative effect on behavioral intention: faculty who worry about learning harms are less likely to adopt, regardless of how easy or useful they perceive ChatGPT to be. Anxiety about misuse was negatively associated with behavioral intention, with significant mediation through both EE and PE: misuse anxiety makes the tool seem harder and less useful, which then reduces adoption intent. Anxiety about professional change did not show a statistically significant relationship with behavioral intention. The findings suggest that targeted interventions must address the specific anxiety types that faculty hold, and that emphasising pedagogy — specifically designing ChatGPT use to enhance rather than replace student learning — may be more effective than technical training alone.
