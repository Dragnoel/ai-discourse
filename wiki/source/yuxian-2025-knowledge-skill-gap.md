Peer-reviewed empirical article in *Computers & Education* (Elsevier, 2025). Jin Yuxian at Sungkyunkwan University, Seoul, reports a 2×2 within-subjects experiment (N=80 undergraduate students) examining the combined effects of LLM chatbot access and critical thinking guidance on declarative and procedural knowledge acquisition across four learning tasks in domains outside participants' major.

## Summary

The study is motivated by a persistent gap in educational outcomes: students typically acquire declarative knowledge (factual understanding, "knowing what") more readily than procedural knowledge (skills and applied understanding, "knowing how"). Yuxian frames this as the "knowledge-skill gap" and investigates whether LLM chatbots and critical thinking guidance — individually and in combination — can address different components of this gap. The theoretical foundation is Cognitive Load Theory, which distinguishes intrinsic, germane, and extraneous cognitive load and predicts different optimal conditions for declarative versus procedural learning.

The 2×2 factorial design crossed two interventions: LLM chatbot access (vs. no access) and critical thinking guidance (vs. no guidance). Eighty undergraduate students completed four learning tasks in unfamiliar domains, with outcomes measured as performance on declarative knowledge tests (factual recall and comprehension) and procedural knowledge assessments (applied problem-solving). Regression models with task and learner fixed effects controlled for confounds.

LLM chatbot access alone significantly improved declarative knowledge acquisition by reducing the cognitive resources needed for information retrieval, allowing available cognitive capacity to support conceptual understanding. However, LLM access did not significantly improve procedural learning, consistent with the prediction that over-reliance on AI reduces the germane cognitive load investment required for schema construction. Critical thinking guidance alone improved procedural learning by activating deeper cognitive processing, but simultaneously increased cognitive load, thereby limiting declarative knowledge gains. The key finding is a synergistic interaction: combining both interventions produced significant improvements in both declarative and procedural knowledge, with the LLM chatbot mitigating the cognitive load imposed by critical thinking guidance and guidance ensuring the productive struggle necessary for procedural learning.
