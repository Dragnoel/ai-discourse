Peer-reviewed empirical article in *Frontiers in Psychology* (February 2026). Anurag Shekhar and Musawenkosi Saurombe at the University of Johannesburg analyse N=1,454 Reddit narratives about AI-driven job displacement through a mixed-methods design combining VADER and BERT sentiment analysis with qualitative thematic analysis. The study introduces and operationalises "algorithmic anxiety" as a syndrome distinct from general technostress, framing it as a violation of the psychological contract between workers and employers.

## Summary

The study is motivated by the observation that AI-driven workplace transformation generates psychological harms that existing organisational frameworks — built on assumptions of human reciprocity between employers and employees — are poorly equipped to address. Standard technostress frameworks identify technology-related stressors (overload, invasion, complexity, insecurity, uncertainty) but do not fully capture the identity and dignity dimensions that emerge when algorithms make decisions previously made by human managers. Shekhar and Saurombe argue that "algorithmic anxiety" is a distinctive syndrome that goes beyond fear of job loss to encompass the dehumanisation of work relationships and the erosion of the implicit contracts that give employment its psychological meaning.

Reddit was selected as a data site because its anonymity and community upvoting structure create conditions for authentic disclosure of workplace experiences that workers feel unable to share through official channels. The 1,454 comments were drawn from a discussion thread explicitly soliciting narratives from workers affected by automation. VADER sentiment analysis detected overall valence, while BERT captured deeper semantic patterns. Network analysis identified three interconnected discourse communities centred on employment disruption, ethical concerns, and technical systems (modularity Q=0.42). Thematic analysis produced seven recurring themes: shattered trust and corporate betrayal, eroded identities, technostress, devalued expertise, anxiety about the future, cynicism about adapting, and affirming human values.

The study's theoretical contribution lies in applying psychological contract theory, conservation of resources theory, self-determination theory, and technostress theory simultaneously to algorithmic management contexts. The implication is that organisations seeking to address algorithmic anxiety should prioritise transparent communication, involve employees in AI governance decisions, and design AI systems that explicitly preserve employee dignity.
