# 4T Pyramid Model

The 4T Pyramid Model is a four-category taxonomy of student metaphors for generative AI in academic writing, developed by Jin et al. (2025) through metaphor analysis of 53 unique metaphors elicited from 277 international postgraduate students across 14 countries. The four categories are: Technical Support, Text Development, Transformative Potential, and Threat.

## The four categories

**Technical Support** (exemplary metaphor: high-heeled shoes). GenAI as a functional aid that makes certain tasks easier or possible but does not change the fundamental nature of the work. Students using this framing see AI as a tool they can put on or take off without transforming their academic identity.

**Text Development** (compass). GenAI as a navigational guide for the writing process — helping students find direction, organise ideas, and improve clarity, without determining the final destination. The compass metaphor foregrounds AI as process support rather than output generator.

**Transformative Potential** (Spider-Man). GenAI as conferring abilities beyond ordinary human capacity — moving through information environments, generating outputs at superhuman speed, solving problems that would be intractable alone. The most positively valenced category; also the most common across the sample.

**Threat** (drug). GenAI as dependency-inducing, voice-eroding, and ethically risky. The drug metaphor captures concerns about loss of original thinking, addiction to AI convenience, and the ethical risk of submitting AI-generated work as one's own.

## Findings

Most students in the sample conceptualised GenAI as primarily Transformative. Disciplinary background significantly shaped metaphor choice: STEM and business students showed stronger Technical Support framings; social sciences and humanities students showed greater ambivalence and more frequent Threat framings.
