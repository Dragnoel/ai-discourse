# Assistance Dilemma

The assistance dilemma is a term attributed to Koedinger and Aleven (2007) for what they describe as a fundamental open problem in instructional science: how much assistance to provide learners, and when to withhold it. Too little assistance produces unproductive frustration and disengagement; too much eliminates the cognitive engagement necessary for genuine learning. The optimal balance depends on learner characteristics (prior knowledge, expertise) and task demands (complexity, cognitive load), and cannot be specified in the abstract.

## Relevance to AI-education discourse

The assistance dilemma is invoked in Bassner et al. (2026) as the conceptual frame for their comparison of scaffolded (Iris) and unrestricted (ChatGPT) AI tutors. The study operationalises the dilemma as a question about AI design: does providing complete code solutions (as ChatGPT does) constitute a form of over-assistance that eliminates productive cognitive struggle, while providing calibrated hints (as Iris does) preserves it? Their findings support this interpretation: ChatGPT conditions showed no learning advantage despite significantly improving exercise performance, while Iris conditions preserved intrinsic motivation.

The assistance dilemma becomes more acute with GenAI because, as Bassner et al. note, LLMs can generate complete solutions effortlessly — unlike earlier intelligent tutoring systems that provided limited, structured hints. The qualitative shift in the nature of available assistance changes the conditions under which the dilemma manifests.
