# Explainable AI

Explainable AI (XAI) refers to the field concerned with making AI system decisions, outputs, or reasoning processes interpretable and understandable to human stakeholders. The precise meaning of "explainability" is contested: different communities define explainability in terms of saliency maps, textual justifications, counterfactual examples, local model approximations, or other representations, and there is no consensus on which method satisfies the goal.

## Definitional pluralism

Ehsan and Riedl (2024) argue, drawing on SCOT, that XAI's definitional pluralism is not a problem to be resolved through standardisation but a condition of its current developmental stage. The two dominant communities — NLP (natural language processing) and CV (computer vision) — have different epistemic cultures and different practical requirements, producing different legitimate interpretations of what explainability must accomplish. Forcing closure through a single monolithic definition would, they argue, stifle innovation and privilege one community's interpretive framework over others.

## Human-Centered XAI (HCXAI)

Ehsan and Riedl (2020, cited in 2024) developed the Human-Centered XAI (HCXAI) approach, which frames XAI challenges as sociotechnical rather than purely algorithmic. HCXAI emphasises that the relevant question is not merely how to open the black box algorithmically but who is doing the opening and for what purpose — different stakeholders need different explanations, and the social context of explanation matters as much as its technical form.
