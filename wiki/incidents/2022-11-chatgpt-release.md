# 2022-11 ChatGPT-3.5 Release

In November 2022, OpenAI released ChatGPT-3.5, a large language model chatbot with a public-facing interface capable of generating human-like text across a wide range of topics and tasks. The release is identified across the majority of sources in this collection as the precipitating event that accelerated research and institutional attention to generative AI in education, the workplace, and public life.

## What happened

ChatGPT-3.5 was made publicly available by OpenAI in November 2022. Within months of its release, millions of learners were using it for homework assistance; universities had moved to ban or restrict it; and the academic literature had begun grappling with its implications for academic integrity, pedagogy, professional roles, and societal change. The pace of adoption was notable for its breadth: unlike prior AI tools that were embedded in specific professional or industrial systems, ChatGPT was immediately accessible to the general public at no cost.

## Significance in this corpus

Nikolic et al. (2024) frame their systematic review of faculty adoption explicitly in relation to the "November 2022 launch of ChatGPT-3.5." Mah and Groß (2024) note that the ChatGPT release "sparked diverse global reactions from the academic community, ranging from outright bans to cautious acceptance and adaptation." Verano-Tacoronte et al. (2025) subtitle their study "Unpacking faculty anxiety in the ChatGPT era," indicating the release as the temporal framing of the research. Bassner et al. (2026) use ChatGPT as one of the three conditions in their RCT, treating it as the reference point for unrestricted AI assistance.

The release is referenced implicitly in virtually every source in this collection that was published post-2022.
