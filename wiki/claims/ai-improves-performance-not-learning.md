Bassner et al. (2026) argue, on the basis of a three-arm RCT in introductory programming education (N=275, TU Munich), that AI assistance improves measurable task performance without producing corresponding gains in conceptual understanding or skill acquisition. The paper is the primary empirical source for what Bassner et al. term the [[Performance-Learning Dissociation]] and the "comfort trap."

## The claim, in their own framing

Bassner et al. argue that "the educational impact of AI depends critically on its pedagogical design, not merely its technical capabilities." Unrestricted AI assistance (ChatGPT) increased exercise scores but showed no statistically significant pre-to-post learning gains relative to control; only scaffolded AI (Iris) increased intrinsic motivation, though without learning advantages either. The authors frame this as evidence that AI tools acting primarily as performance aids create conditions in which "students' preferences misaligned with pedagogical effectiveness."

## Evidence the proponents offer

- RCT (three conditions: ChatGPT, Iris, no-AI control); N=275; 90-minute programming exercise on concurrent Java
- Dependent variables: exercise scores (auto-graded), pre-post knowledge test (code comprehension), cognitive load subscales (intrinsic, germane, extraneous), frustration, intrinsic motivation
- Both AI conditions achieved significantly higher exercise scores; neither showed significant learning gains or code-comprehension improvements vs. control
- Only Iris improved intrinsic motivation; ChatGPT showed no motivational benefit despite higher helpfulness ratings

## Counter-positions
- [[Yuxian (2025)]] finds synergistic effects when LLM assistance is combined with critical thinking guidance — suggesting the dissociation is a design artifact, not an inherent property of AI assistance
- [[Wang et al. (2026)]] find learning and motivation improvements when ChatGPT is embedded in a structured three-framework pedagogical design

## Status
Active. Single high-quality RCT in programming education context; generalisability to other domains and pedagogical designs remains an empirical question.
