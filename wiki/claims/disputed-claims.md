# Disputed Claims

Index page listing claim pairs or groups where sources in this collection take contradictory or directly opposed positions. Each entry links to the relevant claims/ pages. Resolution is deferred to `wiki/_review.md` until sufficient evidence is available.

A claim pair belongs here when: (1) at least two sources in this wiki take opposing positions on the same empirical question or normative framing; and (2) the disagreement cannot be dissolved by noting that the sources study different populations or use different outcome measures.

---

## Active disputes

### 1. Does AI literacy increase or decrease AI adoption?

- **Position A:** Lower objective AI literacy predicts greater AI receptivity, mediated by perception of AI as magical. — [[Lower AI Literacy Predicts Greater AI Receptivity]] (Tully et al. 2024)
- **Position B:** AI literacy (self-reported) positively predicts actual GenAI usage in foreign language learners. — (Xu and Xiong 2026; see source page xu-xiong-2026-genai-acceptance-fl.md)

**Status:** May resolve on construct distinction (objective vs. self-reported literacy; receptivity vs. actual usage; consumer vs. student populations). See `wiki/_review.md` for tracking note.

---

### 2. Do lay judges show algorithm aversion or algorithm appreciation?

- **Position A:** Lay decision-makers weight advice more heavily when it is labeled algorithmic — algorithm appreciation. — [[Lay Decision-Makers Prefer Algorithmic to Human Advice]] (Logg et al. 2019)
- **Position B:** Stated preferences for human vs. algorithmic advice do not predict actual advice uptake. — [[Algorithm Preference Does Not Predict Advice Use]] (Himmelstein & Budescu 2023)
- **Position C:** The field is fragmented; algorithm aversion should be reconceptualised as preference for humans over algorithms, requiring a classification scheme before findings can be compared. — (Jussupow et al. 2024; see source page jussupow-2024-algorithm-aversion.md)

**Status:** Positions A and B may not directly contradict (Logg measures WOA; Himmelstein & Budescu find stated preference ≠ WOA). Position C is meta-level. See [[Algorithm Aversion]] and [[Algorithm Appreciation]] for construct details.
