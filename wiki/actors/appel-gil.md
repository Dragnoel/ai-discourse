# Gil Appel

Gil Appel is a researcher at George Washington University (GWU), Washington DC. In this collection he is a co-author of Tully et al. (2025), a ten-study program demonstrating that lower AI literacy predicts greater receptivity to AI, mediated by perceptions of AI as magical.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Tully, Longoni, and Appel (2025) report a practical implication that is counterintuitive for policy: AI literacy education, typically advocated as a route to informed adoption, may inadvertently reduce the appeal of AI products by demystifying them, without necessarily converting higher-literacy skeptics into users.
