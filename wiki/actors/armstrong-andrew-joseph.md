# Andrew Joseph Armstrong

Andrew Joseph Armstrong is a doctoral researcher who completed a Doctor of Business Administration (DBA) in Information Technology at South College, Knoxville, Tennessee (2025). His dissertation examines how public awareness of AI shapes human decision-making, emotional intelligence, and social behavior in everyday life, using the Social Construction of Technology (SCOT) framework.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Armstrong (2025) applies SCOT to public AI awareness data drawn from the Pew Research Center, finding that socioeconomic position, geographic location, and generational cohort all moderate how people engage with and understand AI in daily life. The study argues that systemic barriers to equitable AI adoption map onto pre-existing social inequalities rather than emerging from AI itself.
