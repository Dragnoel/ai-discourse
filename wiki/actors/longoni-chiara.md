# Chiara Longoni

Chiara Longoni is a researcher at Bocconi University, Milan. In this collection she is a co-author of Tully et al. (2025), which documents the lower AI literacy–greater AI receptivity link in consumer decision-making.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Tully, Longoni, and Appel (2025) show that the literacy–receptivity link is moderated by task type: it attenuates for tasks not associated with distinctively human attributes such as personality or emotion. The mechanism — perceptions of AI as magical — suggests that AI's cultural appeal partly derives from its apparent transcendence of ordinary technology, an effect that literacy dispels.
