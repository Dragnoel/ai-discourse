# Armin Heinzl

Armin Heinzl is a researcher at the University of Mannheim, Germany, working in information systems. In this collection he is a co-author of Jussupow et al. (2024), a research note in *MIS Quarterly* developing an integrative framework for algorithm aversion and appreciation.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Jussupow, Benbasat, and Heinzl (2024) argue that "algorithm" can be understood either as a computational procedure (the traditional view) or as an IT artifact embedded in material or nonmaterial objects, and that this distinction affects how aversion and appreciation are measured. The classification scheme they develop guides researchers toward more specific, testable hypotheses about when preferences favour humans over algorithms.
