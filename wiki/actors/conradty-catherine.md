# Cathérine Conradty

Cathérine Conradty is a researcher at the University of Bayreuth, Germany, affiliated with the DISCOVERY SPACE Erasmus+ project on science education. In this collection she is the lead author of Conradty et al. (2026), a mixed-methods study of 841 European students' conceptions of AI in school contexts.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Conradty et al. (2026) introduce a dual coding framework distinguishing students' functional expectations for AI (System A) from their pedagogical values (System B), yielding three recurrent AI role repertoires — Tutor, Coach, and Companion orientations. The study finds that across all profiles, students maintain a normative boundary between AI that scaffolds thinking and AI that replaces cognitive effort.
