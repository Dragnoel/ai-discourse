# Stephanie Tully

Stephanie Tully is a researcher at the USC Marshall School of Business, University of Southern California. In this collection she is the lead author of Tully et al. (2025 / bib key 2024), a multi-study program of research in the *Journal of Marketing* demonstrating a counter-intuitive negative relationship between objective AI literacy and AI receptivity.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Tully et al. (2025) find that lower objective AI literacy predicts greater AI receptivity, mediated by perceptions of AI as magical and feelings of awe. The study challenges the prevailing assumption that AI literacy education drives informed adoption, noting that demystifying AI may inadvertently reduce its appeal among currently receptive populations.
