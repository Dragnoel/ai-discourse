# Jennifer Logg

Jennifer Logg is a researcher in judgment and decision-making, affiliated with Harvard Kennedy School at the time of this collection's primary source. She is the lead author of Logg et al. (2019), the paper that coined the term "algorithm appreciation" and documented the counter-intuitive finding that lay people weight algorithmic advice more heavily than equivalent human advice.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Logg et al. (2019) — "Algorithm appreciation: People prefer algorithmic to human judgment" — is the primary empirical basis for the claim that algorithm aversion is not the universal phenomenon the prior literature had suggested. The paper is cited by all three algorithm aversion/appreciation sources in this collection (Jussupow et al. 2024; Himmelstein & Budescu 2023) as the key work establishing the appreciation effect. The paradox it identifies — lay people show appreciation while professional forecasters show aversion — has become a reference point for the construct's scope conditions.
