# Rebecca Eynon

Rebecca Eynon is a researcher at the Oxford Internet Institute (OII), University of Oxford, UK, working at the intersection of digital technology, education, and social inequality. In this collection she is the lead author of Eynon and Young (2021), a SCOT-informed interview study examining how academia, industry, and policy stakeholders construct AI for lifelong learning.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Eynon and Young (2021) argues that AI for lifelong learning is constructed through three incompatible technological frames and that the commercial sector's frame dominates AI design, crowding out educational and democratic concerns. The paper is one of three in this collection to apply the [[Social Construction of Technology]] framework to AI discourse (alongside Ehsan & Riedl 2024 and Armstrong 2025).
