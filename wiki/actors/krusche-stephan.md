# Stephan Krusche

Stephan Krusche is a researcher at the Technical University of Munich (TU Munich), Germany, known for work in software engineering education and AI tutoring systems. In this collection he is the senior author of Bassner et al. (2026), the study evaluating scaffolded versus unrestricted AI assistance in an introductory programming course.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Bassner et al. (2026) report experimental findings showing that ChatGPT users cluster at high exercise scores while Iris (scaffolded tutor) users spread across the full performance range — a distributional difference with implications for assessment design in AI-integrated courses. The study motivates assessment approaches that remain diagnostic when performance no longer reliably tracks understanding.
