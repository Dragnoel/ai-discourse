# Julia Minson

Julia Minson is a researcher at Harvard Kennedy School, Cambridge, Massachusetts, working on judgment and decision-making. In this collection she is a co-author of Logg et al. (2019), the paper that coined the term "algorithm appreciation."

## Key contributions to the discourse

Logg, Minson, and Moore (2019) demonstrate across six experiments that lay people assign greater weight to advice when it is labeled algorithmic rather than human — an effect that is robust but bounded by conditions including self-selection and professional expertise. The paper is the primary empirical basis for the appreciation side of the algorithm aversion/appreciation debate in this collection.
