# Yanzhen Lan

Yanzhen Lan is a researcher affiliated with Qingdao University of Science and Technology, China, and the University of Cambridge, UK. In this collection she is the sole author of Lan (2024), a mixed-methods case study examining how teacher professional identity tensions shape motivations to integrate AI in a Chinese university-level teacher training programme.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Lan (2024) applies the IBM-SDT framework (Identity-Based Motivation and Self-Determination Theory) to data from 216 survey participants and 15 in-depth interviews, identifying three TPI tension dimensions (groupness-individuality, humanity-technology, continuity-openness) and three corresponding AI role models (Navigator, Collaborator, Inventor). The study provides an identity-motivation mechanism for understanding faculty AI adoption heterogeneity that standard UTAUT frameworks do not address.
