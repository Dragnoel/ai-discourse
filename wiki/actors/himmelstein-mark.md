# Mark Himmelstein

Mark Himmelstein is a researcher at Fordham University, New York. In this collection he is the lead author of Himmelstein and Budescu (2023), an empirical study funded by IARPA examining forecasting advice-taking in geopolitical and economic contexts.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Himmelstein and Budescu (2023) identify a critical validity problem in algorithm aversion research: stated preferences for human versus algorithmic advice do not predict actual advice use (Weight on Advice). The paper challenges a foundational assumption in the aversion/appreciation literature and argues that studies measuring preferences alone cannot draw conclusions about behavioral outcomes.
