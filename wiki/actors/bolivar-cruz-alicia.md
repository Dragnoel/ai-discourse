# Alicia Bolívar-Cruz

Alicia Bolívar-Cruz is a researcher at a Spanish public university (specific institution not stated in this collection's source material). In this collection she is a co-author of Verano-Tacoronte et al. (2025), a structural equation modelling study of faculty anxiety and ChatGPT adoption intention.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Verano-Tacoronte, Bolívar-Cruz, and Sosa-Cabrera (2025) demonstrate that anxiety about ChatGPT misuse is negatively associated with behavioral adoption intent, with significant mediation through both effort expectancy and performance expectancy. This finding implies that misuse anxiety operates through rational-cognitive channels, making it addressable through training and ethical guidance in ways that other anxiety types may not be.
