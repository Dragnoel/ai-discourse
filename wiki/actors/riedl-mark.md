# Mark O. Riedl

Mark O. Riedl is a researcher at the Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, working on AI and interactive narrative. In this collection he is co-author of Ehsan and Riedl (2024), which argues for productive definitional pluralism in explainable AI (XAI) using the Social Construction of Technology framework.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Ehsan and Riedl (2024) propose a four-point argument: XAI is currently pluralistic; pluralism requires communication precision even without definitional uniformity; SCOT provides tools to understand who is making which claims and why; and the field can progress without monolithic consensus. The piece positions Human-Centered XAI as a sociotechnical approach that foregrounds stakeholder plurality rather than technical uniformity.
