# Kelsey Prena

Kelsey Prena is a co-author of Huang et al. (2025); institutional affiliation is not stated in this collection's source material. The study uses focus groups and polls with 25 undergraduates to examine academic cheating with GenAI.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Huang et al. (2025) extend the Theory of Planned Behavior with a moral obligation construct, finding that personal ethics and peer influence are stronger predictors of GenAI-assisted cheating than formal institutional deterrents. This suggests that intervention strategies focused primarily on detection and punishment may be less effective than those targeting moral norms and social contexts.
