# Patrick Bassner

Patrick Bassner is a researcher at the Technical University of Munich (TU Munich), Germany. In this collection he is lead author of Bassner et al. (2026), a three-arm randomised controlled trial examining the effects of different AI support types on performance, learning, and motivation in an introductory programming course.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Bassner et al. (2026) coin the term "comfort trap" to describe a pattern in which AI assistance improves exercise scores without producing corresponding learning gains. The study demonstrates that scaffolded, hint-first AI design (Iris) preserves motivational benefits that unrestricted AI (ChatGPT) does not, providing experimental evidence for the pedagogical importance of AI design choices.
