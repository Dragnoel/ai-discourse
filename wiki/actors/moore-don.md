# Don Moore

Don Moore is a researcher at the Haas School of Business, University of California, Berkeley, known for work on overconfidence and judgment under uncertainty. In this collection he is a co-author of Logg et al. (2019), which documents the algorithm appreciation effect in lay decision-makers.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Logg, Minson, and Moore (2019) find a paradox at the intersection of expertise and algorithmic trust: the lay people who most benefit from algorithmic guidance accept it, while professional forecasters — the one group who regularly uses forecasting algorithms — resist it, to the detriment of their accuracy. This expert-lay divergence is a key scope condition for the algorithm appreciation construct.
