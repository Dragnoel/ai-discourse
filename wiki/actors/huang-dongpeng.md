# Dongpeng Huang

Dongpeng Huang is a researcher at Boston University, Massachusetts. In this collection he is the lead author of Huang et al. (2025), a mixed-methods study of 25 undergraduate students examining academic cheating behavior with GenAI through a moral extension of the Theory of Planned Behavior.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Huang et al. (2025) find that peer influence and personal ethics are stronger drivers of GenAI-assisted cheating than institutional policies or class-level deterrents, and that students show clear disapproval of overt cheating but considerable ambivalence about subtle forms. The study argues that academic integrity policies targeting obvious misconduct miss the consequential gray areas where GenAI use is most contested.
