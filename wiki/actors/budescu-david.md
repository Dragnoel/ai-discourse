# David Budescu

David Budescu is a researcher at Fordham University, New York, specialising in judgment and decision-making under uncertainty. In this collection he is co-author of Himmelstein and Budescu (2023), a study examining the relationship between stated preferences for algorithmic advice and actual advice uptake.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Himmelstein and Budescu (2023) find that domain-specific stated preferences (human preferred in politics; algorithmic preferred in economics) do not predict persuasiveness in actual decision-making — judges were primarily sensitive to quantitative features of advice (distance from their prior, confidence) rather than to source identity. The study constrains interpretations drawn from preference-only measures in the algorithm aversion literature.
