# OpenAI

OpenAI is a US-based AI research company and the developer of the GPT series of large language models, including ChatGPT. It functions as a commercial vendor and, to a lesser extent, as a research institution. In the sources in this collection, OpenAI appears overwhelmingly as the originating actor behind ChatGPT — the technology that precipitated most of the research documented here — rather than as a named proponent of specific discourse claims.

## Role in the corpus

The November 2022 release of ChatGPT-3.5 is the precipitating incident referenced explicitly or implicitly across the majority of the 24 sources in this collection. Papers spanning EdTech, algorithm aversion, labour, and discourse analysis all date a shift in research attention or practitioner concern to this release. Nikolic et al. (2024) frame their systematic review as a response to ChatGPT's November 2022 emergence; Bassner et al. (2026) use ChatGPT as one of their three experimental conditions; Verano-Tacoronte et al. (2025) examine faculty anxiety "in the ChatGPT era."

OpenAI is not cited in this corpus as a named proponent of specific knowledge claims about AI discourse. Its significance here is primarily as an actor whose technology has structured the research agenda of the field.
