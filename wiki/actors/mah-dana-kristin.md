# Dana-Kristin Mah

Dana-Kristin Mah is a researcher at Leuphana University of Lüneburg, Germany. In this collection she is the lead author of Mah and Groß (2024), a quantitative survey study of 122 faculty members examining AI self-efficacy, perceived benefits and challenges, and professional development needs in higher education.

## Key contributions to the discourse

Mah and Groß (2024) use latent class analysis to identify four distinct faculty profiles — optimistic, critical, critically reflected, and neutral — and find that the relationship between AI self-efficacy and actual use is significant only for the optimistic profile. The study argues that institutional AI support services must be profile-sensitive rather than one-size-fits-all.
