# AI Self-Efficacy

AI self-efficacy is an individual's belief in their capacity to use and interact with AI systems effectively to accomplish specific tasks. It is an adaptation of Bandura's (1977) general self-efficacy construct to the domain of artificial intelligence.

## Definition and scope

AI self-efficacy is treated as a domain-specific form of self-efficacy: not confidence in general technology use, but specifically in AI-mediated tasks. In higher education contexts it has been differentiated into sub-domains — teaching with AI, researching with AI, and professional AI engagement — on the grounds that these make qualitatively different demands on the user.

## Operationalisation

The construct is measured primarily through self-report Likert scales adapted from Bandura-based instruments. Xia et al. (2025) developed and validated the GAICS-TR, the first scale designed specifically for higher education academics, covering four domains and nine dimensions (N=787 across nine universities).

## Cross-source notes
