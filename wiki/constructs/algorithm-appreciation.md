# Algorithm Appreciation

Algorithm appreciation is the tendency to weight algorithmic advice more heavily than equivalent human advice. Logg et al. (2019) coined the term to describe findings that run counter to the received wisdom of algorithm aversion: under many ordinary decision conditions, lay people actually prefer algorithmic to human judgment.

## Definition and scope

Logg et al. (2019) identify the effect most clearly for numeric estimation tasks (weight, song popularity, attractiveness) where no strong personal relationship or subjective domain is involved. The effect attenuates or reverses when: participants choose their own advisor (experts show aversion), the task is a subjective personal preference domain, or participants have relevant domain expertise.

## Relation to algorithm aversion

Algorithm appreciation and [[Algorithm Aversion]] are often framed as opposites, but Jussupow et al. (2024) argue the empirical literature is fragmented because researchers use incompatible definitions, algorithms (computational procedures vs. IT systems), and decision configurations. The same individual can show aversion in one context and appreciation in another.
